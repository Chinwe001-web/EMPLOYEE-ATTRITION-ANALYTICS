# EMPLOYEE-ATTRITION-ANALYTICS

**PROJECT OVERVIEW**

The objective of this analysis is to understand and predict employee attrition of a small scale fictous company having 

a capacity of 100 employees.The executives wants to find out why employees are leaving in large volumes,what factors contribute 

most to their decision to leave,identifying at-risk employees, ways to improve retention strategies and creating a healthier 

and more productive work environment.


**OBJECTIVES**

• Identify key drivers of attrition.

• Determine which departments have the highest attrition and why.

• Build a profile of a typical employee who is at risk of leaving so HR can intervene quickly.

• Use findings to recommend practical ways the company can help improve saatisfaction and reduce turnover.

  **KEY INSIGHTS**

 • Attrition rate is critically high at 65%,leading to a drastical loss of employees.This also inidicates that the company is spending more on recruitment ,onboarding and training often and on.

 • With an average job satisfaction score of 2.4 out of 4, it shows that more than half of the workforce rated ther satisfaction at 1 or 2.Employees with satisfaction score 1 are leaving at a rate of 88% which is bad for business.

 • Overtime is a major driver of attrition .Employees who work overtime are exiting at a rate of 87% compared to 7% of those who do not.This is an alarming finding which the management needs to look into.

 • Employees betwen the ages of 36-45 are leaving at a rate of 51% followed by those between 26-35  at 18%.This points to a failure in reataining early career talents before thez become fully productive contributors.

 • Most employees who leave do so within the first five years at the company .This suggest that the onbaording experience,early career support the company offers are critical important and currently not yielding
 
 • Poor work life balance and far distance from home with attrition rates 78% and 86% respectively are major high risk contributors to employees exiting the company.


**RECOMMENDATIONS**

 • The company should address the overtime problem by reviewing all workloads across all departments especially Sales executives and HR representative roles.Also they should consider hiring additional staff to redistribute the burden evenly.

 • Given that average job satisfaction is 2.4,an anonymous survey should be carried out to find out exactly what is making employees unhappy and address feedbacks quickly so that employees feel heard.

 • Since most attrition happen between the first few years the company should invest in making it positive and enganging by assigning mentors to new hires to make them feel welcomed and supported,giving them a clearer career path.

 • Remote work and flexible work options should be introduced given that distance from home is a significant attrition driver.This would reduce commute related stress at the same time improving work life balance.
 
 • Attrition should be checked and tracked a s a key metric monthly on PowerBI just as Profit or Revenue is tracked.This will help create an early warning indicator for managers when employees’s satisfaction score drops prior to when they decide to leave.
